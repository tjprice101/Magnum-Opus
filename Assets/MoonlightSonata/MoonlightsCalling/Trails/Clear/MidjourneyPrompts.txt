================================================================================
  MOONLIGHT'S CALLING — CLEAR TRAIL TEXTURES
  Asset Category: Transparent-Background Trail Strips for Overlay Rendering
================================================================================

PURPOSE:
  These are the same concept as the Trails/ textures but with fully transparent
  backgrounds instead of black, designed for overlay/alpha-blend rendering rather
  than additive blending. The existing reference assets (GlowTrailClear.png and
  ThinLineGlowClear.png in SandboxLastPrism/Trails/Clear/) show the pattern:
  soft glowing energy forms on transparent/alpha backgrounds with feathered
  edges. These are used when trails need to be drawn with standard alpha
  blending to layer over complex backgrounds without the washed-out look that
  additive blending can cause on bright backgrounds. The vertex trail renderer
  can switch between additive mode (using black-background textures from Trails/)
  and alpha-blend mode (using these transparent textures) depending on the
  rendering context.

TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS:
  - Canvas: 256x64 or 512x128 pixels (horizontal strips)
  - Background: FULLY TRANSPARENT (alpha = 0) — NOT black
  - The colored beam/trail elements must have proper alpha channel falloff
  - Edges must feather to full transparency (alpha = 0) smoothly
  - Colors should be slightly more saturated than the black-background versions
    since they won't benefit from additive blending's brightness boost
  - Top and bottom edges: full transparency with smooth alpha gradient
  - Left edge (trail head): highest opacity, most saturated
  - Right edge (trail tail): fading to transparency
  - Internal detail is important but should be softer than the additive versions

NOTE ON MIDJOURNEY AND TRANSPARENCY:
  Midjourney does not natively output transparent PNGs. These prompts specify
  transparent/alpha backgrounds as a conceptual guide. Post-processing will be
  needed: generate on black, then use Photoshop/GIMP to convert the luminance
  channel to alpha (bright = opaque, dark = transparent), or generate on a
  solid color (like bright green) and key it out. The prompts below request
  black backgrounds for generation purposes with notes on the intended alpha
  conversion.

VISUAL IDENTITY:
  "Prismatic musical refraction — spectral shards, prismatic violet-blue beams, musical
  note motifs, light splitting through crystal. Every beam is moonlight through
  a prism. Playful yet arcane."
