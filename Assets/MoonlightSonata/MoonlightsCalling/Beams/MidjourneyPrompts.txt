================================================================================
  MOONLIGHT'S CALLING — BEAM TEXTURES
  Asset Category: Elongated Beam Strip Textures for Magic Projectile Rendering
================================================================================

PURPOSE:
  These are horizontal strip textures mapped onto beam rendering meshes for the
  MoonlightBeam projectile fired by the Moonlight's Calling tome. In the mod
  code, the beam projectile uses CalamityStyleTrailRenderer.DrawTrailWithBloom()
  with Cosmic style for primary beams and Ice style for child spectral beams.
  BeamTrailWidth() returns 12-22 pixels expanding with bounce count, and
  BeamTrailColor() interpolates from DarkPurple -> Violet -> PrismViolet ->
  RefractedBlue -> IceBlue -> MoonWhite with shifting based on bounceCount.
  These beam textures serve as the PRIMARY visual body of the magic projectile
  — the channeled laser-like beam of refracted moonlight that the tome fires.
  Unlike trails (which render behind the projectile from oldPos[]), these beams
  ARE the projectile body rendered along its forward velocity vector. The beams
  bounce off walls, split into spectral child beams on bounce 3+, and detonate
  in a prismatic cascade on the final bounce. Each bounce increases visual
  intensity (crescendo effect). The textures are UV-mapped where U spans the
  beam length (0=origin, 1=tip) and V spans the beam width (0=top, 1=bottom).

TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS:
  - Canvas: 256x64 or 512x128 pixels (horizontal strips)
  - Background: Solid black (#000000) — rendered with additive blending
  - Left edge (beam origin near tome) should be brightest and most concentrated
  - Right edge (beam tip traveling outward) should taper and dissipate
  - Top and bottom edges must fade to black for smooth beam width blending
  - Internal detail and energy patterns visible when stretched along beam length
  - Should tile or look seamless when UV-scrolled horizontally by shaders
  - Must convey the identity of REFRACTED MOONLIGHT — light that has passed
    through a prism and been split into spectral violet-blue components
  - Color palette: night void (40,10,60), dark purple (75,0,130),
    violet (138,43,226), prism violet (160,80,255), refraction lavender (180,130,255),
    refracted blue (100,200,255), ice blue (135,206,250),
    spectral cyan (110,220,255), tome silver (190,200,255),
    moon white (240,235,255)

VISUAL IDENTITY:
  "Prismatic musical refraction — spectral shards, prismatic violet-blue beams,
  musical note motifs, light splitting through crystal. Every beam is moonlight
  through a prism. Playful yet arcane."

CALAMITY STYLE REFERENCES:
  Study channeled beam projectiles from Subsuming Vortex and Vivid Clarity for
  beam rendering quality, energy density, internal turbulence patterns, and how
  magic beams convey concentrated arcane power through elongated strip textures.
