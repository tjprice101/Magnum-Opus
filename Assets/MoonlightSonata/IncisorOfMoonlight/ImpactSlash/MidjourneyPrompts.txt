NOTE ON INCISOR IMPACT IDENTITY:
The Incisor of Moonlight's impacts are NOT heavy blunt-force explosions or
wide shockwaves. They are precise, concentrated, surgical strike effects —
star-burst point impacts like a constellation node detonating, crystalline
fracture patterns radiating from the exact point of contact, tight frequency
shockwave rings that expand with mathematical precision. Every hit should
feel calculated and exact, not chaotic or explosive. The impact says
"I struck the exact point I intended to strike."

PROMPT 1 — CONSTELLATION NODE DETONATION: Star-Burst Point Impact
================================================================================
PURPOSE: The primary Incisor hit effect — the impact point erupts like a
constellation node star igniting. A precise point source of brilliant light
with radiating lines in constellation-geometry angles. The hit IS a star
being born at the point of the blade.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A 2D visual effects texture of a concentrated melee weapon hit impact that erupts as a constellation star-node detonation at a single precise point, rendered on a solid pure black RGB(0, 0, 0) background for additive blending in a 2D game engine. The impact is centered on a singular epicenter point that blazes with extraordinary concentrated brilliance — the very center is a tight, compact core of Moon White RGB(240, 235, 255) intensity that is almost overexposed in its luminance, a pinpoint of stellar ignition no more than a few pixels across, representing the exact point where the blade's edge made contact. Immediately surrounding this core, Harmonic White RGB(235, 240, 255) energy expands into a tiny but intensely bright halo that transitions through Crystal Edge RGB(220, 230, 255) and into Resonant Silver RGB(230, 235, 255) with a subtle metallic flash quality — the halo is tight and controlled, not a wide explosion but a contained blast radius, the light of a star concentrated rather than dispersed. Radiating outward from this central star-point, a precisely arranged array of geometric burst lines extends in multiple directions, but unlike a generic radial starburst, these lines follow constellation-geometry angles — they do not spray outward in uniform radial symmetry but instead extend at specific angles that suggest the node points and connection lines of constellation diagrams, some lines longer and brighter than others, creating an asymmetric but mathematically intentional pattern. The primary burst lines — four to six dominant rays — are rendered in Ice Blue Clarity RGB(135, 206, 250) with Crystal Edge RGB(220, 230, 255) cores, extending the farthest from the epicenter and tapering from bright thick bases to impossibly thin points as they radiate outward. Secondary burst lines fill the gaps between primary rays with thinner, shorter lines of Frequency Pulse Lavender RGB(170, 140, 255) and Lavender RGB(180, 150, 255), each line perfectly straight and ruler-precise, not curved or organic in any way. Tertiary micro-lines, barely visible, extend at additional angles in Violet RGB(138, 43, 226), filling out the constellation detonation pattern. At the terminal points of several primary burst lines, small secondary star-nodes ignite — tiny concentrated points of Harmonic White RGB(235, 240, 255) brilliance that echo the central star, as though the impact energy has propagated along constellation connection lines and activated adjacent star-nodes in the celestial network, each secondary node one-third to one-quarter the size and intensity of the primary epicenter. Thin connecting lines of Lavender RGB(180, 150, 255) link these secondary nodes back to the central impact point in straight geometric paths, completing the constellation diagram. The outermost extent of the impact energy fades through Violet RGB(138, 43, 226) into Deep Resonance Purple RGB(90, 50, 160) and finally into Night Void RGB(40, 10, 60) at the very tips of the farthest rays. The overall impact reads as a single stellar point igniting and instantaneously mapping its local constellation connections — precise, sharp, geometric, beautiful.
