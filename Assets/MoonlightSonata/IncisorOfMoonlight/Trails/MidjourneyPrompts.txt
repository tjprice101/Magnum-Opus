 INCISOR OF MOONLIGHT — TRAIL TEXTURES
 Midjourney Prompt Reference
================================================================================

PURPOSE:
  Elongated horizontal strip textures mapped onto vertex-mesh trail geometry
  by the CalamityStyleTrailRenderer system. The trail renderer builds a
  triangle strip mesh following the projectile/blade path, then UVs are
  mapped so that:
    U (horizontal, 0-1) = position along the trail (0 = head/newest, 1 = tail)
    V (vertical, 0-1) = position across the trail width (0 = top edge, 1 = bottom)

  The IncisorResonance.fx and MoonlightTrail.fx shaders sample these textures
  with distorted UVs to create their visual effects. Key shader behaviors:
  - Standing wave displacement (perpendicular oscillation of V coordinate)
  - High-frequency shimmer (rapid U scrolling)
  - Constellation node highlights (bright spots at regular U intervals)
  - QuadraticBump edge fade (bright center, fading edges across V)
  - White-hot core masking (brightest along V=0.5 centerline)

  The renderer makes 3 passes per trail with different color pairs, so
  these textures are drawn multiple times with additive blending.

TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS:
  - Dimensions: 256x64 (standard) or 512x128 (high-detail)
  - Horizontal orientation: Left = trail head (bright), Right = trail tail (fading)
  - Vertical structure: Bright center band, fading to dark/transparent top and bottom
  - Background: Black (#000000) where the trail fades out
  - Format: PNG (alpha optional since black = invisible in additive blend)
  - Must tile VERTICALLY without visible seam (top and bottom edges match)
  - Should NOT tile horizontally (head and tail are intentionally different)
  - Internal detail must survive UV distortion by the shader

COLOR PALETTE (Incisor of Moonlight — "The Stellar Scalpel"):

 POST-PROCESSING NOTES:
  - Resize to exact power-of-two dimensions (256x64 or 512x128)
  - Verify that top and bottom edges are pure black (#000000)
  - The shader applies its own edge fade via QuadraticBump, so the texture's
    vertical fade acts as a multiplier on top of the shader's — keep the
    texture's V-fade relatively gentle to avoid double-darkening
  - The shader scrolls the U coordinate (distortedUV.x += shimmerDisp) so
    fine horizontal detail will animate and shimmer during gameplay
  - The shader adds its own constellation node highlights via the
    ConstellationNode() function, so the texture's nodes are supplementary
  - Test with the IncisorResonance.fx shader parameters:
      uScrollSpeed: 1.0-2.0
      uDistortionAmt: 0.04-0.08
      uIntensity: 0.3-1.0 (varies with combo step)
  - The CalamityStyleTrailRenderer.TrailStyle.Cosmic uses a specific UV
    mapping that stretches the texture along curved trail geometry — test
    your texture on curved paths, not just flat strips
  - Trail textures are rendered 3 times per trail at different scales and
    colors, so subtle internal detail is important for visual richness
