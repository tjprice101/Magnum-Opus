================================================================================
  RESURRECTION OF THE MOON -- MUZZLE FLASH ASSETS
  Midjourney Prompt Reference
================================================================================

PURPOSE:
  Muzzle flash and barrel discharge textures drawn at the weapon's muzzle
  point when the gun fires. These are short-lived additive-blended sprites
  (2-4 frames visible) that convey the violent celestial energy release of
  each comet round being launched. They should feel like a concentrated
  crescent-moon-shaped energy eruption -- not a conventional gunpowder flash
  but a celestial discharge event with comet-launch plasma, crescent arc
  geometry, and concentrated blue-white detonation light.

USAGE IN MOD:
  - Primary muzzle flash sprite drawn at the barrel tip each time the weapon
    fires, randomly selected from the set and randomly mirrored vertically
    for variety
  - Scaled muzzle bloom overlay (larger, softer version drawn behind the
    sharp flash for glow diffusion)
  - Rapid-fire burst flash variant (compact, bright, less elaborate for when
    the weapon fires at maximum rate)
  - Charged shot release flash (larger, more dramatic for fully-charged
    comet rounds)
  - Crescent-shaped directional muzzle flare for the weapon's signature
    crescent moon barrel aperture

FORMAT REQUIREMENTS:
  - 128x128 or 256x256 PNG
  - Pure black (#000000) background (additive blend discards black)
  - The flash should be oriented with the primary energy direction pointing
    RIGHT (toward the target) since the weapon fires rightward; the engine
    will rotate to match actual aim direction
  - Registration point (visual origin of the flash) should be at the LEFT
    CENTER of the image (where the barrel muzzle would be)
  - Energy should erupt rightward and slightly spread vertically, but the
    leftward back-blast should be minimal (20-30% of the rightward extent)
  - All edges must feather to pure black; no energy touching the image borders
  - sRGB color space, 8-bit per channel, no embedded ICC profile

COLOR PALETTE:
  Deep Space Violet ........... RGB(50, 20, 100)
  Impact Crater Indigo ........ RGB(100, 80, 200)
  Comet Trail Lavender ........ RGB(180, 120, 255)
  Lunar Shine Blue ............ RGB(120, 190, 255)
  Comet Core Pale Blue ........ RGB(210, 225, 255)
  Blue-White Impact ........... RGB(235, 240, 255)

  POST-GENERATION NOTES:
  - Upscale all results to 256x256 for primary use; keep 128x128 downscaled
    versions for the rapid-fire variant (#3) and bloom overlay (#5) where
    smaller texture resolution is acceptable
  - Verify pure black (#000000) in all four corners and along all borders;
    repaint any near-black Midjourney artifacts to true black
  - CRITICAL REGISTRATION: The flash origin point (where the barrel muzzle
    meets the flash) should be at approximately pixel coordinates (32, 128)
    for a 256x256 texture, or (16, 64) for 128x128. Adjust the generated
    image position if the origin drifts from left-center
  - In-engine compositing order for a single weapon fire frame:
    1. Draw the Lunar Veil Bloom (#5) behind everything at 50-70% opacity
    2. Draw the primary or variant flash (#1, #2, or #3) on top at full opacity
    3. For charged shots, draw Supernova Discharge (#4) replacing the primary
  - The Crescent Moon Flare (#2) is the weapon's SIGNATURE flash and should
    appear most frequently during normal fire; the standard Celestial Discharge
    (#1) fills in for variety on alternating shots
  - Rapid-Fire (#3) replaces the primary flash when the weapon fires faster
    than 10 shots per second to prevent visual clutter from overlapping flashes
  - The Supernova Discharge (#4) should only appear for fully charged shots
    or critical hit shots; its intensity warrants a brief screen-shake in code
  - All flashes should be randomly mirrored on the vertical axis each time
    they appear to prevent repetitive visual patterns
  - Consider generating 2-3 variants of prompts #1 and #3 to have a pool of
    slightly different flashes for random selection during sustained fire
