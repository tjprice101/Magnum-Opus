================================================================================
  RESURRECTION OF THE MOON -- IMPACT BURST ASSETS
  Midjourney Prompt Reference
================================================================================

PURPOSE:
  Explosion, detonation, and impact burst textures rendered at the point where
  comet projectiles strike enemies or terrain. These are the climactic visual
  payoff of the weapon's firing cycle -- the comet round completes its
  trajectory and detonates with devastating celestial force. Each burst is a
  short-lived additive-blended sprite sequence (3-8 frames visible depending
  on variant) drawn centered on the collision point. They must convey the
  violence of a celestial body impacting with supernova force: crater
  detonations, shockwave expansions, debris fragmentation fields, and
  concentrated blue-white annihilation light.

USAGE IN MOD:
  - Primary impact explosion when standard comet rounds hit an enemy or tile
  - Crater detonation burst for ground-impact strikes with expanding ring
  - Supernova crit burst for critical hit strikes (scaled up 2-3x with
    slower fade and screen shake)
  - Comet fragmentation debris field for multi-hit splash damage visualization
  - Resurrection ultimate impact -- the most devastating detonation when the
    weapon's ultimate ability projectile strikes

FORMAT REQUIREMENTS:
  - 128x128 or 256x256 PNG (larger variants at 512x512 for the ultimate)
  - Pure black (#000000) background (additive blend discards black)
  - Centered radial composition -- the detonation epicenter must be at the
    exact center of the image
  - No energy touching the image borders; all effects must feather to pure
    black with generous margin
  - These may be asymmetric and chaotic (explosions are messy) but the visual
    center of mass must remain at image center for correct registration
  - sRGB color space, 8-bit per channel, no embedded ICC profile

COLOR PALETTE:
  Deep Space Violet ........... RGB(50, 20, 100)
  Impact Crater Indigo ........ RGB(100, 80, 200)
  Comet Trail Lavender ........ RGB(180, 120, 255)
  Lunar Shine Blue ............ RGB(120, 190, 255)
  Comet Core Pale Blue ........ RGB(210, 225, 255)
  Blue-White Impact ........... RGB(235, 240, 255)

  POST-GENERATION NOTES:
  - Export Prompts #1, #2, and #4 at 256x256 for standard resolution
  - Export Prompt #3 (Supernova Crit) at 256x256 but also generate a 512x512
    variant for extreme close-up encounters or boss fights
  - Export Prompt #5 (Resurrection Ultimate) at 512x512 minimum; this texture
    will be scaled up significantly in-engine and needs maximum detail
  - Verify pure black (#000000) in all corners and along all borders; repaint
    any near-black Midjourney artifacts to true black; this is especially
    critical for #5 whose outermost halo may produce near-black values that
    are not exactly #000000
  - CRITICAL CENTER REGISTRATION: The visual epicenter of each explosion must
    be at the exact center pixel of the image. If Midjourney drifts the center
    of the explosion, reposition the image in post so the brightest point is
    at (128, 128) for 256px or (256, 256) for 512px
  - In-engine draw order for a single impact event:
    1. Draw primary explosion (#1 or #3 for crits) at collision point, full size
    2. On the same frame, draw a 2x scaled copy of the same texture at 30%
       opacity behind it for bloom
    3. On the next frame, draw the Crater Ring (#2) at 80% opacity expanding
       outward (scale increasing over 4-6 frames)
    4. For splash hits, draw the Fragmentation Field (#4) at the same collision
       point with a 1-frame delay
    5. For ultimate impacts, draw #5 at 1.5-2x scale with a 12-frame fade
  - The Fragmentation Field (#4) individual fragment positions should be
    randomized in the draw code by rotating the texture randomly each spawn
  - For the Resurrection Ultimate (#5), the vertical pillar element may need
    a separate draw pass or a custom shader to enhance the pillar glow
    independent of the radial explosion scaling
  - All impact bursts should trigger a screen-shake in code; recommended
    intensities: #1 = 2px for 4 frames, #2 = 3px for 6 frames, #3 = 5px
    for 8 frames, #4 = 2px for 4 frames, #5 = 8px for 15 frames
  - Consider generating the Flare assets (in Flare/ folder) as companions
    to these bursts -- draw a matching flare on top of the explosion center
    for extra brightness and light-ray detail
